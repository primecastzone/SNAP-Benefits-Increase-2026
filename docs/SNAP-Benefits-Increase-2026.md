## What Is SNAP?

The **Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP)**, formerly known as Food Stamps, is a federal nutrition assistance program that helps eligible low-income individuals and families purchase food. Benefits are distributed through an Electronic Benefit Transfer (EBT) card and can be used at approved retailers and grocery stores nationwide.

SNAP plays an important role in reducing food insecurity and supporting households facing financial challenges.

## Who May Qualify?

Eligibility for **SNAP Benefits 2026** is generally based on:

* Household income
* Household size
* Citizenship or qualifying immigration status
* Work requirements (when applicable)
* Resources and deductions

Most states also use special eligibility rules for seniors, disabled individuals, and certain low-income households.

## How to Apply for SNAP Benefits

Applying for SNAP is usually a straightforward process.

### Step 1: Complete an Application

Submit an application through your state SNAP office or online benefits portal.

### Step 2: Provide Documents

You may need:

* Government-issued identification
* Proof of income
* Proof of address
* Social Security information
* Household expense records

### Step 3: Attend an Interview

Most applicants are required to complete a phone or in-person interview.

### Step 4: Receive a Decision

State agencies review applications and determine eligibility.

### Step 5: Receive Your EBT Card

Approved applicants receive an EBT card that can be used to purchase eligible food items.

## What Can SNAP Benefits Buy?

SNAP benefits can generally be used for:

* Fruits and vegetables
* Bread and cereals
* Meat and poultry
* Dairy products
* Snacks and non-alcoholic beverages
* Seeds and plants that produce food

Benefits generally cannot be used for alcohol, tobacco products, vitamins, supplements, or most prepared hot foods.

## SNAP Benefits for Seniors

Many older adults qualify for SNAP but never apply. Seniors may be eligible for special deductions related to medical expenses, which can help increase benefit amounts. In some states, simplified application procedures are available for eligible senior households.

## Common Reasons SNAP Applications Are Denied

Applications may be denied because of:

* Missing documents
* Income exceeding program limits
* Failure to complete an interview
* Incomplete application information
* Ineligibility under program rules

Applicants who are denied may have appeal rights through their state agency.
